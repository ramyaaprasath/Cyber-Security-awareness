Example: testing.in, tester.inc


 Website spoofing is the creation of a replica of a trusted site with the intention of misleading visitors to a phishing site. Website spoofing attacks have become increasingly prevalent in recent years. Even unsophisticated attackers can register a domain that's very close to the domain of a trusted brand and build a site that looks identical to the brand's website. Then, using phishing emails, attackers can lure the brand's customers, partners, and others to the site and trick them into revealing sensitive information.
